Ankit Chakraborty

Window

2CEP

I look at the broken window of the house of a maiden widow.
I ponder about the resemblance of me and the broken window.
I see myself in the window.
Transparent like it.
Easy to be
shattered like it.
Bit foggy and unclear at times.
The thing that draws the most similarity between me and the window are
the cracks and fractures we have in common.
These fractures
have broken us as a whole, segmented into pieces.
Each fragmented piece of me grew up to something.
A new me, a person I don't even know.
Each trait magnified like a TV character and becoming a character of it's own.
You have the angry one, the broken one, the anxious- restless one, the
quick witted one, the stoop down low for others, one.
Each fragment grew.
They want to take over the body as a whole.
The chaos goes on.
The fight between all the traits goes on as if it's the families from The Game of Thrones. Chaos unbearable,
Fractures forever to stay,
The battle forever to be with me.
